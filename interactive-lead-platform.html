"It's too expensive"
⭐ 94%
💰 PRICE
STEP 1: ACKNOWLEDGE
"I completely understand - this is an important financial decision and you want to make sure you're getting value for your money."
STEP 2: REFRAME
"Rather than thinking about the cost, let's look at what you're actually getting. This is $4.50 a day - less than your morning coffee - but it protects your family's entire financial future. If something happens tomorrow, your family gets $500,000. Can you really put a price on that peace of mind?"
STEP 3: CLOSE
"What's more expensive - $4.50 a day now, or your family struggling financially if something unexpected happens? The question isn't whether you can afford it, it's whether you can afford not to have it."
💡 Pro Tips:
• Break down to daily cost ($135/mo = $4.50/day)
• Compare to everyday expenses (coffee, Netflix, dining out)
• Emphasize consequence cost (medical bills, lost income)
• Use silence after the close - let them process
"I need to think about it"
⭐ 89%
⏰ TIMING
STEP 1: ACKNOWLEDGE
"Of course! This is a big decision and you should take the time you need. Can I ask - what specifically would you like to think about?"
STEP 2: REFRAME
"Here's what I've learned in this industry: the best time to get insurance was 10 years ago. The second best time is today. Every day you wait, you're one day older, and premiums only go up. Plus, if something happens to your health tomorrow, you might not qualify at all."
STEP 3: CLOSE
"Let me ask you this - if we started the application today and got you locked in at today's rate, you'd still have a 14-day free-look period to change your mind. But if you wait and something happens, there's no going back. What makes more sense?"
💡 Pro Tips:
• Uncover the real objection with "what specifically?"
• Create urgency with age/health angle
• Use free-look period as safety net
• Ask assumptive closing question

"I'm young and healthy, I don't need it yet"
⭐ 91%
❓ NEED
STEP 1: ACKNOWLEDGE
"That's actually exactly WHY you need it now! You're making the smartest decision at the perfect time."
STEP 2: REFRAME
"Think about it - when you're young and healthy, premiums are lowest and you're guaranteed to qualify. Every year you age, prices go up 3-5%. And if you develop any health condition later - diabetes, high blood pressure, anything - you either pay 2-3x more or get rejected entirely. Insurance isn't for when you need it; it's for when you can GET it."
STEP 3: CLOSE
"My job is to insure healthy people - because that's who insurance companies WANT. Once you're sick, it's too late. Lock in your health and your rate today, while you still can."
💡 Pro Tips:
• Flip the script - young = perfect timing
• Explain compound cost of waiting
• Use health change scenarios (everyone gets older)
• Create urgency with "while you can" framing
"I found a cheaper plan online"
⭐ 87%
⚔️ COMPETITOR
STEP 1: ACKNOWLEDGE
"Great! I love that you're comparing options - that's exactly what you should do. Can I see what you found? Let's make sure we're comparing apples to apples."
STEP 2: REFRAME
"Here's what most people miss: cheaper premiums usually mean lower coverage, shorter terms, or exclusions in the fine print. AIA includes critical illness riders, hospital cash, and worldwide coverage as standard. Most 'cheaper' plans charge extra for those, and suddenly we're actually more affordable. Plus, we've paid out claims for 50+ years - can that online company say the same?"
STEP 3: CLOSE
"Insurance isn't about who's cheapest - it's about who pays when you need them. Would you rather save $20 a month now, or have $200,000 MORE coverage when it matters? What's your family's financial security worth?"
💡 Pro Tips:
• Ask to see competitor quote (often incomplete)
• Compare coverage details, not just price
• Emphasize company stability & track record
• Reframe value vs. cost
"I need to discuss with my spouse first"
⭐ 90%
👥 DECISION
STEP 1: ACKNOWLEDGE
"Absolutely! This affects your whole family, so it's important you're both on the same page. That's a sign of a smart partnership."
STEP 2: REFRAME
"Can I ask - what do you think they'll want to know? Usually it's coverage amount, cost, and how it protects the family. I can explain it to both of you together, or I can give you this simple one-pager that breaks everything down. Which would work better?"
STEP 3: CLOSE
"Here's my suggestion: let's pencil in a 15-minute call with both of you tomorrow. I'll answer all their questions directly, and you can both decide together. Does morning or evening work better for your spouse?"
💡 Pro Tips:
• Validate need for spousal buy-in
• Offer joint meeting (higher close rate)
• Provide takeaway materials
• Schedule specific next step (don't leave open-ended)
"I can't afford it right now"
💰 PRICE
STEP 1: ACKNOWLEDGE
"I hear you - budgets are tight for everyone right now. Let me ask you something though..."
STEP 2: REFRAME
"If you can't afford $135 a month now, how would you afford a $150,000 medical bill or 20 years of lost income if something happens? The truth is, you can't afford NOT to have this. We can start with basic coverage now and upgrade later - but you need SOMETHING protecting your family."
STEP 3: CLOSE
"Let me show you our entry-level plan - same protection, smaller coverage amount, under $100/month. Where can you cut $3 a day? One less grab food order? That's all it takes to protect your family."
💡 Pro Tips:
• Reframe affordability question (cost of NOT having)
• Offer scaled-down options
• Find budget cuts together ($3/day angle)
• Start small, upgrade path later
"My company already provides insurance"
❓ NEED
STEP 1: ACKNOWLEDGE
"That's great your company provides coverage - shows they value you! But let me share three critical gaps most people don't realize..."
STEP 2: REFRAME
"Gap #1: Company coverage is usually 1-2X your salary - is that enough to replace 20 years of income? Gap #2: It only covers YOU, not your spouse or kids. Gap #3: If you leave the job, get retrenched, or retire, you lose everything. This policy is yours for life, regardless of your employment."
STEP 3: CLOSE
"Think of company insurance as your foundation and this as your walls and roof. Together, they give you complete protection. Plus, you own this one - nobody can take it away. Makes sense?"
💡 Pro Tips:
• Never attack company insurance (they value it)
• Educate on 3 gaps (amount, dependents, portability)
• Use metaphor (foundation + walls = complete home)
• Emphasize ownership and permanence
